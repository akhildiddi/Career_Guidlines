ITI (Industrial Training Institute)

ITI stands for Industrial Training Institute, which is a vocational training institute that offers various technical courses to students who have completed their secondary education (10th standard). These courses aim to provide students with practical skills and knowledge in various trades such as electrician, fitter, welder, machinist, mechanic, and so on. ITIs are recognized by the government of India and provide certification upon completion of the course. The eligibility criteria for ITI courses vary depending on the course and the state in which the institute is located. In general, students who have completed their secondary education (10th standard) are eligible to apply for ITI courses. Some courses may require students to have specific subject combinations in their secondary education. In addition to the basic eligibility criteria, some ITIs may also have additional requirements, such as age limits, physical fitness requirements, and language proficiency. Students are advised to check with the specific ITI they are interested in to know the exact eligibility criteria and admission requirements. Overall, ITI courses provide a great opportunity for students who want to pursue a career in the technical field. These courses not only provide students with practical skills but also offer them better job prospects in various industries
